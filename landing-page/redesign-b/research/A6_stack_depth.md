# Course 1 Preflop (PPWM) — A6: Stack Depth (Shallow vs Deep)

## Why stack depth changes everything
PokerCoaching’s position/stack depth guide makes the core point cleanly:
- Preflop sets “range, pot size, and plan for later streets.”
- Two biggest levers: **position and stack depth**.  
Source: https://pokercoaching.com/blog/preflop-strategy-guide-mastering-position-and-stack-depth/

## Shallow stacks (e.g., <40bb): simplify + prioritize top-pair value
PokerCoaching’s summary:
- “Short stacks (under 40bb): favor hands that make top pairs or strong equity, such as Broadway cards and pairs… avoid speculative holdings.”  
Source: https://pokercoaching.com/blog/preflop-strategy-guide-mastering-position-and-stack-depth/

**Course phrasing**
- With shallow stacks, you don’t have enough room to “maneuver” postflop.
- Implied odds shrink → small pairs / suited connectors lose value.

## Deep stacks (100bb+): implied odds + disguised monsters matter more
PokerCoaching’s summary:
- “Deep stacks (100bb+): play hands that can hit hidden monsters like suited connectors and small pairs.”  
Source: https://pokercoaching.com/blog/preflop-strategy-guide-mastering-position-and-stack-depth/

**Course phrasing**
- When stacks are deep, the goal isn’t just “make a pair.” It’s “make a hand that can win a *stack*.”

## Stack depth & preflop sizing systems (practical defaults)
PokerCoaching’s preflop chart page includes a simple sizing system (useful as a consistent default):
- Open raise: **2.5x**
- 3-bet IP: **3x** the open
- 3-bet OOP: **3.5x** the open
- 4-bet IP: **2.5x** the 3-bet
- 4-bet OOP: **2.75x** the 3-bet  
Source: https://pokercoaching.com/preflop-charts

**Teaching note**: Even if players adjust sizes, the key is consistency + understanding how stack depth drives commitment.

## Quick “what changes” bullets for A6
- **Range composition**: shallow = more high-card strength; deep = more suited/connectivity.
- **Aggression/commitment**: shallow = fewer streets, more all-in equity; deep = more postflop leverage.
- **Punishment for mistakes**: deep stacks amplify reverse implied odds (dominated top pair can lose a stack).

## Suggested structure for A6 lesson page
1. Define effective stack depth.
2. Shallow: value-first, less speculation.
3. Deep: implied odds hands enter ranges.
4. Mention a consistent sizing system.
5. 3 quick examples (how a hand changes value from 25bb to 100bb).
