# Course 1 Preflop (PPWM) — A4: Blind Defense (BB/SB)

## Why blind defense is tricky
- You’re out of position, with less information, and (often) a capped range.
- But you also get **great pot odds** in the BB, so folding too much gets punished.

## Pot odds & defense frequency: the math lens
A clean way to explain “defend enough” is **Minimum Defense Frequency (MDF)**:
- MDF = **pot / (pot + bet)** (minimum % of range you must continue with to prevent automatic profit from bluffs).  
Source: BetMGM poker guide (includes MDF definition + formula + examples)  
https://poker.betmgm.com/en/blog/poker-guides/minimum-defense-frequency-vs-pot-odds-in-poker/

**Teaching use**: Joan can introduce MDF as “guardrails,” but emphasize exploitative adjustments are fine vs opponents who don’t bluff enough.

## Big blind defense: the “equity realization” lens
PokerCoaching’s multiway BB defense article is gold because it quantifies pot odds and then explains why **equity ≠ EV** in the blinds:
- Example (heads-up): facing a **2.5bb** open, calling **1.5bb** more; the article computes needing **~27% equity** HU.  
- Example (multiway): if opener raises 2.5bb and BTN calls, BB gets better raw pot odds (needs ~19% equity), **but** many offsuit hands realize poorly because they’re dominated by *two* ranges and make weak one-pair hands.  
Source: PokerCoaching — “Defending The Big Blind In Multi-Way Pots”  
https://pokercoaching.com/blog/defending-the-big-blind-multiway/

Key quote-worthy concept:
- “Most players defend wider when more players are in the pot due to pot odds, where they should really be defending tighter or at least be more selective…”  
Source: https://pokercoaching.com/blog/defending-the-big-blind-multiway/

## Practical blind-defense rules (course-friendly)
### 1) Heads-up BB vs open: defend wide, but with *playable* hands
- Suited hands, connected hands, and hands that can make strong draws generally realize equity better.

### 2) Multiway BB: tighten the offsuit trash (even if pot odds look amazing)
- Avoid weak offsuit hands that mostly make “one pair and pray.”

### 3) Don’t overuse MDF vs weak opponents
BetMGM warns MDF isn’t always the best tool when:
- Opponent “cannot have natural bluffs” / doesn’t bluff enough
- You should shift to exploit: fold more vs under-bluffers.  
Source: https://poker.betmgm.com/en/blog/poker-guides/minimum-defense-frequency-vs-pot-odds-in-poker/
