# Course 1 — Preflop — D3: Big Blind Defense Speed Drill (vs BTN/CO Opens)

**Purpose:** Stop bleeding blinds by (1) understanding the price you’re getting, and (2) building a *fast* BB defend strategy: **3-bet / call / fold**.

## Assumptions
- 6-max NLHE cash, 100bb, no ante.
- Folds to **BTN** or **CO** who opens.
- Hero is **BB**.
- Default open size in the drill: **2.5bb** (some questions use 2.0bb or 3.0bb).

## Why BB defense is structurally wide (quick math)
When facing small opens you’re getting a price. Example math commonly shown in BB-vs-BTN discussions:
- If BTN min-raises to **2bb**, the pot becomes **2.5bb** (including SB). BB calls **1bb** to win **2.5bb**, which is **3.5:1** pot odds (needs only **~22.2% equity** to call). https://www.vip-grinders.com/poker-strategy/defend-big-blind-vs-button/

Also: “Minimum defense frequency (MDF) describes the portion of your range that you must continue with when facing a bet… to prevent your opponent from profiting by always bluffing.” (Concept reference; here we apply the spirit of it preflop vs steals.) https://upswingpoker.com/minimum-defense-frequency-vs-pot-odds/

> Training note: we’re not trying to compute perfect MDF preflop. We’re training **don’t overfold** + defend with hands that *realize equity*.

---

## How to run the speed drill
- Use a metronome/timer: **2–4 seconds per hand**.
- Student answers: **3-BET / CALL / FOLD**.
- If 3-bet, student states a size (bb).

### Default BB 3-bet sizing
- Versus 2.5bb open: **10–12.5bb** (≈4–5×)
- Versus 2.0bb open: **8–10bb**
- Versus 3.0bb open: **12–15bb**

(Heuristic aligns with common guidance that OOP 3-bets are larger than IP.)

---

## Scoring rubric (Green / Yellow / Red)
Per hand:
- **Green (1.0):** matches answer key.
- **Yellow (0.5):** close deviation (call vs mix 3-bet, fold vs mix call).
- **Red (0.0):** clear leak (overfolding strong defend candidates; calling hands with terrible realization vs large size; random 3-bet spew).

Add-on (optional): **+0.25 bonus** if correct 3-bet sizing band.

## Answer key (baseline)
> This is a **teaching baseline**: defend reasonably wide vs BTN, a bit tighter vs CO; 3-bet value + some bluffs.

### vs BTN 2.5bb
1) AKo — **3-bet (value)**
2) AQo — **CALL** (mix 3-bet sometimes; baseline call)
3) A5s — **MIX** (call or 3-bet bluff)
4) A2s — **MIX** (call or 3-bet bluff)
5) KQs — **CALL** (mix 3-bet)
6) KQo — **CALL**
7) KJs — **CALL**
8) KTo — **CALL** (borderline; tends to realize OK vs wide BTN)
9) QJs — **CALL**
10) QJo — **CALL** (borderline but usually defend vs BTN)
11) JTs — **CALL**
12) JTo — **MIX** (call or fold depending on pool; baseline mix)
13) T9s — **CALL**
14) T8s — **CALL**
15) 98s — **CALL**
16) 97s — **MIX** (often call, but easy to over-defend)
17) 76s — **CALL**
18) 65s — **CALL**
19) 55 — **CALL** (some mixes 3-bet; baseline call)
20) 22 — **CALL** (vs small opens; fold more vs larger)
21) A9o — **FOLD** (dominated, poor realization OOP)
22) K9o — **FOLD**
23) Q9s — **CALL**
24) 87o — **FOLD**

### vs CO 2.5bb
25) AKo — **3-bet (value)**
26) AJo — **FOLD** (or very tight mix call; baseline fold)
27) A5s — **MIX** (as bluff 3-bet sometimes; baseline mix)
28) KQs — **CALL** (mix 3-bet)
29) KJo — **FOLD** (dominated more vs tighter CO)
30) QTs — **CALL** (suited, playable)
31) 99 — **CALL** (sometimes 3-bet; baseline call)
32) 76s — **CALL** (playable suited connector; don’t overdo offsuit ones)

### Size changes
33) BTN 2.0bb, J9s — **CALL** (price is better)
34) BTN 2.0bb, 54s — **MIX** (call often; fold if postflop weak)
35) BTN 3.0bb, KTo — **FOLD** (worse price; dominated)
36) CO 3.0bb, A8s — **CALL** (still playable; can fold if pool is tight)

**Sizing key (if 3-bet):**
- vs 2.5bb open: **10–12.5bb**
- vs 2.0bb open: **8–10bb**
- vs 3.0bb open: **12–15bb**

## Coaching feedback patterns

### Leak 1 — Overfolding vs BTN
**Signal:** Folding lots of suited/connectors and broadways vs BTN 2–2.5x.
**Coach:**
- “You’re getting a price. If you fold everything but premiums, BTN steals at will.”
- “Defend with hands that can make pairs + draws; dump dominated offsuit junk.”

### Leak 2 — Defending trash, not playability
**Signal:** Calling hands like K9o/A9o/QTo-o types.
**Coach:**
- “Offsuit dominated hands look ‘close’ but realize like garbage OOP.”

### Leak 3 — No size-based adjustment
**Signal:** Same defend range vs 2x and 3x.
**Coach:**
- “Bigger open = worse price = defend tighter. Don’t autopilot.”

### Leak 4 — BB 3-bet range has no bluffs
**Signal:** Only 3-bets QQ+/AK.
**Coach:**
- “Add a few suited wheel aces (A5s/A4s etc.). They block continues and have playability.”

### Leak 5 — BB 3-bet range is pure chaos
**Signal:** Random 3-bets with offsuit junk.
**Coach:**
- “Bluffs need blockers + playability. If you can’t explain why it’s a bluff, it’s not a bluff — it’s a donation.”
