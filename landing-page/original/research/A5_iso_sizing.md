# Course 1 Preflop (PPWM) — A5: Isolation (ISO) Raising & Sizing

## What ISO raising is
- ISO = **“isolation”**: raising after someone limps with the goal of playing a bigger pot (ideally **heads-up**) versus the weakest player.
- 888poker describes the purpose clearly: “increase the number of pots we play against the weakest preflop limpers… objective is to isolate… in a heads-up pot.”  
Source: https://www.888poker.com/magazine/improve-iso-raising-poker-strategy

## Why ISO sizing matters
The sizing has two jobs:
1. **Punish limping ranges** (make them pay to see flops)
2. **Reduce multiway** (charge the blinds/field so you aren’t playing 4 ways)

## Default ISO sizing guidance (with adjustment levers)
### 888poker (conceptual framework)
888poker gives a “default recommendation” concept and then lists factors for bigger/smaller:
- ISO **larger** when:
  - you’re “constantly getting multiple callers” (e.g., live)
  - you have a **value hand** and villain “hardly ever folds preflop”
- ISO **smaller** when:
  - “effective stacks are shallow”
  - you have a speculative holding and want to prevent the BB from “freerolling” a cheap flop  
Source: https://www.888poker.com/magazine/improve-iso-raising-poker-strategy

### Smart Poker Study (classic rule-of-thumb)
- Smart Poker Study explicitly references the “old wisdom” of ISO sizing as: **“3bb + 1bb per limper.”**  
Source: https://smartpokerstudy.com/how-to-target-limpers-for-fun-and-profits/

**How Joan can present it**
- Online/tougher games: start around **3bb + 1bb per limper**.
- Live/loose games: be ready to go bigger (the goal is isolation; if 3 people call anyway, size up).

## ISO raising ranges (what to teach)
888poker: “Decent ISO raising ranges are similar to RFI ranges but usually slightly tighter.”  
Source: https://www.888poker.com/magazine/improve-iso-raising-poker-strategy

**Course translation**
- ISO tighter than RFI because:
  - limper’s range is wide (you’re not stealing; you’re building a pot vs a caller)
  - you may be OOP more often
  - multiway risk rises

## Biggest mistakes to call out
- ISO too small → invites everyone in, defeats isolation.
- ISO too wide OOP → you build a big pot with hands that can’t realize equity.
- Ignoring players behind (3-bet squeezers): 888poker notes you must consider aggressive 3-betters behind.  
Source: https://www.888poker.com/magazine/improve-iso-raising-poker-strategy

2. Give 1 sizing rule-of-thumb.
3. Give 3 adjustment knobs (callers, value vs speculative, stack depth).
4. Ranges: “like RFI, slightly tighter.”
5. Common mistakes.
