# 30-Day Preflop Plan (Habits > Heroics)

## Intro
Preflop happens every hand. That’s why tiny upgrades compound fast.

The goal of this plan is not “memorize everything.” It’s **purposeful practice**: focus on one spot, get feedback, track mistakes, repeat.

MTT Poker School’s “10-minute training plan” is a clean framework built around:
- **Focus**
- **Feedback**
- **Clearly defined goals**

---

## Key rules
### 1) Train one spot at a time
If you try to “study preflop” as one big blob, you’ll learn nothing and feel busy. Pick a single recurring decision (BTN RFI at 75bb, SB 3-bet vs BTN, etc.).

### 2) Use a tiny daily template (10 minutes)
MTT Poker School’s suggested split:
1. **Study the range** + bucket hands — 3 min
2. **Drill ~100 hands** in that spot — 4 min
3. **Review mistakes + notes** — 3 min

### 3) Track what you’re actually fixing
You need a scorecard. Otherwise you’re just consuming content.

## The 30-day schedule
Use standard preflop charts as your baseline reference for opens/sizing.

### Week 1 (Days 1–7): RFI fundamentals
- Days 1–3: BTN RFI at one stack depth (ex: 30bb or 75bb)
- Days 4–5: CO RFI
- Day 6: HJ/LJ RFI
- Day 7: Review: write the **bottom-of-range** hands for each position

### Week 2 (Days 8–14): 3-bets
- Days 8–10: IP 3-bet spots (BTN vs CO/HJ)
- Days 11–13: OOP 3-bet spots (SB vs BTN/CO)
- Day 14: Sizing consistency + notes
  - IP ~3x, OOP bigger baseline

### Week 3 (Days 15–21): Facing 3-bets
- Days 15–17: IP defense (suited broadways, suited aces, suited connectors)
- Days 18–20: OOP defense tightening (cut dominated offsuit broadways)
- Day 21: Opponent adjustments (tight vs aggressive 3-bettors)

### Week 4 (Days 22–30): Blinds + ISO + stack depth integration
- Days 22–24: BB defense heads-up (pot odds discipline)
- Days 25–26: BB defense multiway: tighten/select for equity realization
- Days 27–28: ISO raising sizing + tighter-than-RFI range concept
- Days 29–30: Stack depth review: shallow vs deep hand class changes

## Examples (how to use the plan)
### Example 1 — A clean Day 1 session
- Spot: BTN RFI (75bb)
- Study: identify bottom-of-range suited hands and offsuit folds
- Drill: 100 hands
- Review: write 3 hands you got wrong and *why*

### Example 2 — Fixing a leak
If your note says: “I defend too wide OOP vs 3-bets,” then Week 3 should be mostly OOP defense drills until accuracy improves.

---

## Common mistakes
- **Skipping review.** Drilling without feedback just ingrains errors faster.
- **Changing three things at once.** You can’t tell what worked.
- **No measurable goal.** If you can’t track it, you can’t improve it.
- **Binge studying once a week.** Ten minutes daily beats 2 hours once.

---

## Drills / checklists
### Daily checklist (copy/paste)
- [ ] Pick one spot (position + stack depth + matchup)
- [ ] 3 minutes: identify 3 bottom-of-range decisions
- [ ] 4 minutes: drill 100 hands
- [ ] 3 minutes: review and write 1 rule
- [ ] Log accuracy %

### Weekly checkpoint
- What spot improved the most?
- What is still your #1 preflop leak?
- What single rule will you focus on next week?

---

## Variance-aware disclaimer
A training plan won’t eliminate downswings. It reduces the frequency of self-inflicted wounds. Your winrate improves by being consistently less wrong, not by having a magical week where every 60/40 holds. Track accuracy and process first; results follow later (and not on your schedule).
