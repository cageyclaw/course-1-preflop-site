# Course 1 Preflop (PPWM) — A1: RFI (Raise First In)

## What “RFI” is (and why it matters)
- **RFI = “raise first in”**: the opening raise when action folds to you. Your RFI range is the baseline that shapes everything else (steals, 3-bets, postflop).  
- Preflop charts are commonly taught as a **starting point**: “hands you should be opening in various situations … broken down … by position” and stack depth.  
  - Source: PokerCoaching “Preflop Charts” page (includes position-by-position opens and sizing heuristics)  
  https://pokercoaching.com/preflop-charts

## Core teaching points Joan can write from
### 1) Position drives range width
- Later position = more profitable opens because you act later postflop and can pressure blinds. (PokerCoaching explicitly frames position as the key driver: play tight EP, widen as you move later.)  
  Source: https://pokercoaching.com/blog/preflop-strategy-guide-mastering-position-and-stack-depth/

**Simple phrasing**: “UTG is a job interview. Button is a party.”

### 2) Use charts as *structure*, not a prison
- PokerCoaching publishes example **GTO open ranges at 75bb** (e.g., UTG ~13.7%, BTN ~54.8%) as a reference baseline.  
  Source: https://pokercoaching.com/preflop-charts
- Good beginner implementation: **memorize ‘cutoff hands’ / bottoms of ranges** by position so you can reconstruct the full range faster, rather than brute-force memorization of every combo.  
  Source (study method article): https://pokercoaching.com/blog/how-to-study-preflop-ranges-and-poker-strategies/

### 3) Default open sizing (keep it consistent)
- PokerCoaching’s exploitative chart page recommends a **default open size** of **2.5x** when raising first (as part of their exploitative chart notes).  
  Source: https://pokercoaching.com/preflop-charts

**Practical note**: Consistent sizing prevents “tells” (big = strong, small = weak).

## Quick heuristics (actionable)
- **EP**: open tight; prioritize hands that make strong top pair / overpairs.
- **LP (CO/BTN)**: widen with playable hands (suited, connected, wheel aces) because position helps equity realization.
- **Table quality matters**: vs tight blinds, steal more; vs sticky callers, tighten the weakest opens and value-open stronger.

## Mini-examples (one-liners)
- “CO/BTN opens include many suited kings/queens and suited connectors that are folds UTG.”
- “If blinds fold too much, your BTN RFI is an ATM.”

## Suggested structure for A1 lesson page
