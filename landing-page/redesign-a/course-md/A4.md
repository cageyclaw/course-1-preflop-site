# Blind Defense (SB/BB)

## Intro
Blinds are the only seats where you **pay to play** every orbit. Defending too tight bleeds chips. Defending too loose creates miserable out-of-position spots.

This section gives you two lenses:
1. **Pot odds / MDF (math guardrails)**
2. **Equity realization (the part people ignore)**

---

## Key rules
### 1) Pot odds tell you the minimum equity you *need*
PokerCoaching illustrates the BB pot-odds idea with a simple heads-up example:
- Facing a **2.5bb** open, BB calls **1.5bb** more.
- You need roughly **~27% equity** heads-up (as computed in their example).

### 2) MDF is a guardrail, not a religion
Minimum Defense Frequency (MDF) is often defined as:
- **MDF = pot / (pot + bet)**

Use MDF to understand “how exploitable” folding is in theory.

### 3) Equity is not EV in the blinds (equity realization matters)
PokerCoaching warns that in multiway pots, players often defend wider because pot odds look better, but actually should be **more selective** because many hands realize equity poorly when dominated by multiple ranges.

### 4) Heads-up BB vs open: defend wide, but prioritize playability
Hands that tend to realize better:
- suited hands
- connected hands
- hands that can make strong draws

### 5) Multiway BB: tighten the offsuit trash
Even if the math says you “have equity,” weak offsuit hands often make weak one-pair hands and get owned by two players.

### 6) Exploit note: fold more vs under-bluffers
BetMGM notes MDF breaks down when opponents don’t bluff enough; against under-bluffers, folding more can be correct.

## Examples
### Example 1 — Heads-up defend
CO opens 2.5x, folds to BB.
- BB can defend fairly wide, especially with suited/connectors that play well.

### Example 2 — Multiway trap
HJ opens 2.5x, BTN calls, BB is tempted to call with **K7o** because pot odds look great.
- This is exactly where equity realization crushes you: you make weak pairs and get dominated by two ranges.

### Example 3 — Exploit adjustment
If the opener is a “never bluffs, always value” type:
- You can **overfold** compared to MDF and feel fine about it.

---

## Common mistakes
- **Defending too wide multiway** just because pot odds look good.
- **Treating MDF as mandatory** vs players who never bluff.
- **Calling with hands that make dominated one-pair hands** (offsuit junk, weak kickers).
- **No plan postflop.** Preflop calls without a postflop strategy become automatic losses.

---

## Drills / checklists
### BB defense checklist
- Heads-up or multiway?
- Am I defending a hand that can make strong draws / robust pairs?
- Will I be dominated often if I make top pair?
- Is villain capable of bluffing enough to justify MDF-style defense?

### Practice drill: “Multiway filter”
Review 20 multiway BB defenses.
- For each, label it:
  - **Good** (suited/connectivity, clear plan)
  - **Marginal** (may be ok HU, questionable multiway)
  - **Trash** (offsuit dominated one-pair hands)

Write one rule you’ll use next session (e.g., “Cut most offsuit Kx/Qx multiway”).

---

## Variance-aware disclaimer
Blind defense is where you’ll feel “picked on” because you’re constantly OOP and constantly paying. The goal isn’t to win every defended pot—it’s to avoid bleeding and to choose hands that realize well. Expect short-term ugliness; track long-run results and, more importantly, decision quality.
