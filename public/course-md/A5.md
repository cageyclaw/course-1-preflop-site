# Isolation (ISO) Raising & Sizing

## Intro
ISO raising means raising after someone limps with the goal of **isolating the weakest player**, ideally creating a heads-up pot where you have an edge.

888poker describes the objective clearly: increase the number of pots you play against weak limpers and isolate them heads-up.

---

## Key rules
### 1) ISO sizing has two jobs
1. **Punish limpers**: make them pay to see flops.
2. **Reduce multiway**: charge the blinds/field so you’re not playing 4 ways.

### 2) Default sizing rule-of-thumb
A classic heuristic:
- **ISO size = 3bb + 1bb per limper**

![ISO sizing ladder](/course-md/iso-sizing.svg)

### 3) Adjust bigger/smaller based on game texture
888poker suggests sizing up when:
- You keep getting multiple callers
- You have a value hand and villain rarely folds
And sizing down when:
- Effective stacks are shallow
- You have a speculative holding and want to avoid giving BB a cheap “freeroll”

### 4) ISO ranges: like RFI, but usually tighter
888poker’s guidance: ISO ranges are similar to RFI but generally **slightly tighter**.
Why?
- You’re not stealing; you’re building a pot vs a caller.
- You may end up OOP.
- Multiway risk goes up.

### 5) Respect the players behind (squeeze risk)
If there are aggressive 3-bettors behind you, your ISO range should tighten and your hand selection should improve.

## Examples
### Example 1 — Online-ish default
One limper, you’re on the BTN.
- Start at **~4bb** (3bb + 1bb).
- If blinds/limper call too much anyway, increase until you reduce the herd.

### Example 2 — Loose live game
One limper, two sticky blinds.
- If 4bb gets three callers, your “ISO” is not isolating.
- Size up (often significantly) to target heads-up pots.

### Example 3 — Speculative hand, shallow stacks
One limper, you have a suited connector, stacks are short.
- Smaller ISO can be reasonable to avoid bloating a pot without implied odds.

---

## Common mistakes
- **ISO too small** → invites everyone in; defeats the whole point.
- **ISO too wide OOP** → you build big pots without position and suffer.
- **Ignoring squeeze players behind.** You ISO, then face a 3-bet you can’t defend.
- **Thinking limpers are “weak” in every way.** Some limpers trap; most don’t, but respect evidence.

## Drills / checklists
### ISO sizing checklist
- How many limpers?
- Are blinds sticky? (If yes, size up.)
- Am I likely to get 3-bet squeezed behind?
- Is my goal heads-up? If not, why am I raising?

### Quick drill: “ISO audit”
Review 20 limped pots you played.
For each:
- Did you ISO?
- What size?
- How many callers?
- Was that outcome acceptable (heads-up / manageable multiway)?

Write one sizing adjustment rule for your pool.

---

## Variance-aware disclaimer
ISO raising increases pot size against players who call a lot—so you’ll have sessions where you isolate correctly, get called, miss the flop, and feel like you’re lighting money on fire. That doesn’t mean ISO is wrong; it means you’re playing higher-variance, higher-edge pots. Track whether your sizes are achieving the goal (isolation) and whether your range is tight enough OOP.
