# Facing a 3-Bet (Defending vs 3-Bets)

## Intro
When you open and face a 3-bet, the hand changes instantly:
- The pot grows fast
- **SPR drops**
- Villain’s range gets tighter
- **Equity realization drops**, especially out of position

PokerCoaching summarizes these exact problems as the core reason you need a plan, not vibes.

---

## Key rules
### 1) You have three options: fold, call, 4-bet
Most hands that open preflop will **fold** versus a reasonably strong 3-bet. That’s not weakness—that’s math.

### 2) What makes a good *call* vs a 3-bet
PokerCoaching’s “best calling hands” bucket is very teachable:
- **Suited broadways** (KQs, QJs, JTs)
- **Suited aces** (AJs down to A2s)
- **Pocket pairs in position**
- **Strong suited connectors** (98s–76s)

### 3) Avoid dominated offsuit broadways (common leak)
PokerCoaching explicitly warns against calling 3-bets with offsuit hands like:
- **AJo, KQo, KJo**
They get dominated too often and realize equity poorly in big pots.

### 4) Position rule: defend wider IP, much tighter OOP
“Position is critical” is not a slogan—it’s the whole defense strategy.

### 5) Opponent adjustments
- Versus **tight 3-bettors**: fold more, continue tighter.
- Versus **aggressive/wide 3-bettors**: call/4-bet more.

## Examples
### Example 1 — IP call candidate
You open CO, BTN 3-bets.
- **KQs** is a classic call: suited, connected, makes strong draws and decent top pair.

### Example 2 — Offsuit trap hand
You open HJ, BTN 3-bets.
- **KQo** looks pretty, but calling often leads to dominated top pairs and ugly turns/rivers.

### Example 3 — Tight vs aggressive 3-bettor
Same hand, different opponent:
- Against a nit: fold more, 4-bet bluff less.
- Against a maniac: widen continues; some hands upgrade from fold → call or 4-bet bluff.

---

## Common mistakes
- **Calling too wide OOP** because “pot odds.” Your equity doesn’t realize.
- **Defending with dominated offsuit broadways** (the most common pain leak).
- **No 4-bet bluffs ever** (makes you easy to 3-bet).
- **Overreacting to one showdown.** “He had it” doesn’t mean your defense is wrong.

---

## Drills / checklists
### vs 3-bet rapid-fire drill
Pick one common spot (e.g., CO open vs BTN 3-bet) and run 100 hands.
- Track three buckets: **Fold / Call / 4-bet**
- Write down the 10 hands you’re unsure about.

### Call vs 3-bet checklist
- Am I in position?
- Does my hand avoid domination?
- Does it flop nut draws or disguised value?
- Is villain 3-betting tight or wide?

### 4-bet checklist
- Am I 4-betting for value, or as a bluff with blockers?
- If I get jammed on, do I have a plan?

---

## Variance-aware disclaimer
Defending vs 3-bets is where variance gets spicy: you’ll sometimes fold the best hand, and sometimes call correctly and still lose a big pot. That’s the cost of playing a game with incomplete information. Grade yourself on **range discipline + position awareness + opponent-adjusted plans**, not on whether the last 30 minutes felt unfair.
