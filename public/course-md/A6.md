# Stack Depth (Shallow vs Deep)

## Intro
**Effective stack depth** (how many big blinds you and villain can actually play) changes:
- which hands are profitable preflop
- how aggressively you can apply pressure
- how painful mistakes become

PokerCoaching frames **position + stack depth** as the two biggest preflop levers.

![Stack depth impact](/course-md/stack-depth-scale.svg)

---

## Key rules
### 1) Shallow stacks (≈ under 40bb): simplify, prioritize top-pair value
PokerCoaching’s summary:
- Short stacks favor **Broadways and pairs**
- Avoid too many speculative hands

Why: with fewer chips behind, you don’t have room for fancy postflop maneuvering, and implied odds shrink.

### 2) Deep stacks (100bb+): implied odds + disguised monsters matter
PokerCoaching’s summary:
- Deep stacks reward hands that can make hidden strong hands (suited connectors, small pairs)

Why: when stacks are deep, the goal is often to win a **stack**, not just a pot.

### 3) Consistent sizing systems help your whole game
PokerCoaching’s chart page includes a simple sizing system you can adopt as a baseline:
- Open: **2.5x**
- 3-bet IP: **3x** the open
- 3-bet OOP: **3.5x** the open
- 4-bet IP: **2.5x** the 3-bet
- 4-bet OOP: **2.75x** the 3-bet

Even if you adjust, keep sizes consistent and understand how stack depth affects commitment.

## Examples
### Example 1 — Small pair value changes
You’re dealt **55** in late position:
- At 25bb: less implied odds; it’s harder to win a stack when you flop a set.
- At 150bb: set-mining becomes more attractive (bigger reward when you hit).

### Example 2 — Suited connector value changes
You’re dealt **98s**:
- Shallow: fewer turn/river resources; you may not get paid when you hit.
- Deep: strong draws + disguised straights can win big pots.

### Example 3 — Dominated top pair becomes expensive deep
Hands like **KJo** can feel okay shallow, but deep stacks amplify reverse implied odds when you make top pair with a weak kicker.

---

## Common mistakes
- **Playing the same ranges at every stack depth.** The game is not static.
- **Overvaluing speculative hands when shallow.** “But it’s suited” doesn’t pay rent at 20bb.
- **Overvaluing one-pair hands when deep.** Deep = more ways to lose a stack.
- **Random sizing changes** when stacks change. Use a system and adjust deliberately.

---

## Drills / checklists
### Stack depth preflop checklist
- What is the effective stack (in BB)?
- Does my hand win mainly by making top pair (good shallow) or by implied odds (good deep)?
- If I get 3-bet, do I have enough depth to call profitably?
- Are my sizes consistent with my system?

### Drill: “Same hand, three depths”
Pick 10 hands (mix broadways, suited connectors, pairs).
For each, write your default action at:
- 25bb
- 75bb
- 150bb

This forces you to see how hand class value shifts.

---

## Variance-aware disclaimer
Stack depth doesn’t just change EV—it changes variance. Deep stacks create bigger pots and bigger mistakes (yours and theirs). Shallow stacks create more all-in situations and higher short-term volatility. Don’t let short-term outcomes convince you to abandon correct stack-depth adjustments.
