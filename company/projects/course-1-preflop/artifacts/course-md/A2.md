# 3-Bet Strategy

## Intro
A **3-bet** is the re-raise versus a preflop open. In modern poker, 3-betting is the main way you:
- Build pots with strong hands
- Pressure opponents off equity
- Prevent people from realizing their range too cheaply

> Reference: Upswing Poker — “3-Bet Preflop Strategy & Range Charts” (summary below)

---

## Key rules
### 1) 3-bet for two reasons: value + pressure
Upswing’s core framing:
- **Value:** build pots with hands likely to win at showdown.
- **Semi-bluff / pressure:** sometimes win the pot now *when opponents overfold*, or deny equity and improve your range’s EV when you get called.

### 2) Understand linear vs polarized ranges
Upswing definitions:
- **Linear 3-bet range:** premiums + strong hands + decent hands that benefit from protection.
- **Polarized 3-bet range:** premiums + semi-bluffs.

Simple teaching rule:
- Use **linear** when you expect **calls**.
- Use **polar** when you expect **folds**, especially **in position**.

### 3) Course default sizing (PokerCoaching-style): IP smaller, OOP bigger
**This course uses one sizing system (100bb-ish):**
- **In position (IP): 3x** the open
- **Out of position (OOP): 3.5x** the open
- Baseline reference: standard preflop chart systems

![3-bet sizing quick guide](/course-md/3bet-sizing.svg)

**Small adjustments (don’t freestyle):**
- If there are callers and you’re squeezing, size up.
- If villain massively over-folds and you’re IP, you can sometimes size slightly smaller.

Rule: **don’t size based on hand strength**.

## Examples
### Example 1 — Linear 3-bet (expecting calls)
You’re OOP vs a player who opens and calls 3-bets too often. Your 3-bet range should skew **stronger and more linear** (more hands that are happy getting called).

### Example 2 — Polar 3-bet (expecting folds)
You’re on the BTN vs a CO who opens wide but folds too much to 3-bets. Polarize: strong value + some bluffs with blockers/playability.

### Example 3 — Sizing discipline
CO opens to 2.5x.
- BTN 3-bet size (IP): ~7.5x total (3x)
- SB 3-bet size (OOP): bigger (3.5x–4.5x depending on system)

## Common mistakes
- **Never 3-bet bluffing (in any spot).** If you only 3-bet premiums, opponents can overfold and play too comfortably against you. (At micros you’ll still be value-heavy; just don’t be *zero* bluffs forever.)
- **Merged 3-bets OOP vs tight opens.** You inflate pots with dominated hands.
- **Sizing tells.** Big with AA/KK, small with bluffs = you’re broadcasting.
- **Forgetting position.** IP can 3-bet wider; OOP needs more discipline and better playability.

## Drills / checklists
### “Three columns” range drill
For one matchup (e.g., BTN vs CO), build three lists:
1. **3-bet for value**
2. **3-bet as bluff**
3. **Call (mostly IP)**

### 3-bet decision checklist
- Am I IP or OOP?
- Does villain open-fold a lot (polar) or open-call a lot (linear)?
- If called, does my hand play well postflop?
- Is my 3-bet sizing consistent with my system?

---

## Variance-aware disclaimer
3-betting creates bigger pots, so swings feel louder. Your bluffs will get called sometimes; your value will get cracked sometimes. Don’t judge your strategy by a one-session graph—judge it by whether your ranges and sizes are coherent and repeatable over thousands of hands.
