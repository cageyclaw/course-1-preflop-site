# Course 1 — Preflop — D1: RFI Decision Lab (Position-Based Open/Fold)

**Purpose:** Build fast, boringly-correct Raise-First-In (RFI) decisions by position.

## Assumptions (so the answer key is deterministic)
- Format: **6-max NLHE cash**, **100bb** effective.
- No ante. Blinds: 0.5bb/1bb.
- Everyone folds to Hero.
- Default open size: **2.5bb** (you can swap sizes later; the *hands* are the point today).
- Goal: “solid baseline” (not trying to max exploit, not pure solver cosplay).

## How to run the lab
- Present 1 hand at a time: **Position + hand**.
- Student must answer in **≤ 5 seconds**: **OPEN** or **FOLD**.
- Optional (if you want tiering): allow **MIX** for borderline hands.

## Scoring rubric (Green / Yellow / Red)
Use this mapping per question:
- **Green (1.0):** matches answer key exactly.
- **Yellow (0.5):** reasonable “mix” deviation:
  - If answer key says **MIX** and student chooses OPEN or FOLD.
  - If answer key says OPEN but student FOLDS a *close* open (flagged as “thin”).
- **Red (0.0):** clear leak:
  - Opening hands that are marked **FOLD** (too loose).
  - Folding hands that are marked **OPEN** and are *core* opens (too tight).

**Grade bands (suggested):**
- **90–100%:** Ready to move on (speed + accuracy).
- **75–89%:** Needs reps (usually too tight late position or too loose early).
- **<75%:** Rebuild RFI foundation; drill slower.

## Coaching feedback patterns (auto-generated style)
Use these as “if you see X, say Y” coaching macros.

### Pattern A — Too loose early position
**Signal:** Opening lots of UTG/HJ hands marked FOLD/MIX (e.g., KQo UTG, T9s UTG).
**Coach:**
- “Early position isn’t a creativity contest. Tighten the offsuit broadways and low suited connectors.”
- “If you want extra hands, add them on BTN first — not UTG.”

### Pattern B — Too tight late position
**Signal:** Folding many BTN/CO opens that are marked OPEN.
**Coach:**
- “Button is the license to print. Your winrate comes from *volume* of steals + position.”
- “If you’re scared postflop, simplify: c-bet good boards, give up bad ones — but keep opening.”

### Pattern C — Confusing suited vs offsuit
**Signal:** Treating KTo and KTs the same, or A8s and A8o the same.
**Coach:**
- “Suitedness buys playability + equity realization. Offsuit hands get dominated and realize worse.”

### Pattern D — Pocket pair fear
**Signal:** Folding mid pairs (55–99) in CO/BTN/SB.
**Coach:**
- “Pairs don’t need to ‘hit a set’ to be profitable opens late. They play fine as high-card bluff-catchers too.”

### Pattern E — Overusing ‘MIX’
**Signal:** Student answers MIX for everything.
**Coach:**
- “MIX is for the edge cases. You still need a default. Pick OPEN or FOLD unless you can name the factor that flips it.”
