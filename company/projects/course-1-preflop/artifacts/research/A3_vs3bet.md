# Course 1 Preflop (PPWM) — A3: Facing a 3-bet (vs 3-bet)

## The problem to solve
When you open and get 3-bet, the hand changes:
- Pot gets big fast
- **SPR drops**
- Villain’s range is tighter
- **Equity realization drops**, especially out of position  
Source: PokerCoaching 3-bet calling ranges article (explicit bullet list)  
https://pokercoaching.com/blog/3-bet-calling-ranges/

## The 3 options (and what decides them)
1. **Fold** (most of your opens should fold vs strong/tight 3-bets)
2. **Call** (select hands that realize equity well)
3. **4-bet** (value + bluffs with blockers, depending on opponent)

PokerCoaching frames the key as: “Knowing which hands to continue with, why they belong in your range, and how to adapt to different opponents…”  
Source: https://pokercoaching.com/blog/3-bet-calling-ranges/

## What makes a good *call* vs 3-bet
PokerCoaching’s “best calling hands” summary is very teachable:
- **Suited broadways** (e.g., KQs, QJs, JTs)
- **Suited aces** (AJs down to A2s)
- **Pocket pairs in position**
- **Strong suited connectors** (e.g., 98s–76s)  
Source: https://pokercoaching.com/blog/3-bet-calling-ranges/

### Hands to avoid calling (common leak)
- “Avoid calling with offsuit broadways like **AJo, KQo, KJo**—dominated too often and realize equity poorly.”  
Source: https://pokercoaching.com/blog/3-bet-calling-ranges/

### Position rule (the course-friendly version)
- PokerCoaching is explicit: “**Position is critical: call wider in position, tighten significantly out of position.**”  
Source: https://pokercoaching.com/blog/3-bet-calling-ranges/

## Opponent adjustments (simple exploit layer)
PokerCoaching’s adjustment line is also copy-ready:
- “Tighten vs tight 3-bettors; call and 4-bet more vs aggressive ones.”  
Source: https://pokercoaching.com/blog/3-bet-calling-ranges/

Upswing echoes the idea from the 3-bettor’s perspective (useful as “why this works”):
- vs players who “rarely fold to 3-bets,” 3-bet more linearly (stronger).
- vs aggressive 4-bettors, cut bottom hands that can’t continue vs 4-bets.  
Source: https://upswingpoker.com/3-bet-strategy-aggressive-preflop/

## Suggested structure for A3 lesson page
1. What changes when facing a 3-bet (SPR, ranges, equity realization).
2. The decision tree: fold/call/4-bet.
3. Call selection: suitedness/connectivity/position.
4. “Never-call” bucket (dominated offsuit broadways).
5. Adjustments vs tight vs aggressive 3-bets.

## Quick checklists Joan can include
**Call vs 3-bet checklist**
- Am I in position?
- Does my hand avoid domination?
- Does it flop nut draws / disguised value?
- Is villain 3-betting tight or wide?

**Typical folds (population)**
- Offsuit broadways that look pretty but play terrible in big pots OOP.
